Class of 1990

Vel Anthony

Vel died as a result of a single car accident in the summer of 1989, never returning to graduate with the inaugural class.

Vel Anthony was probably one of the easiest going people of that first class. No matter where he is was or what he was doing Vel was smiling. And that first year smiling was sometimes rare. Even in the middle class if you looked at him he would be smiling. The first year PE was a requirement. Vel was my doubles partner in tennis lessons. We SUCKED. But he could always make me laugh about how bad we were (and I can be competitive). Vel was a big loss to our class.

Alice (Thompson) Wurst, Class of 1990.

Class of 1992

Aliess Adams

Aliess was born George Randall Adams to George and Shirley Adams. She graduated from LSU in 1998 where she studied Studio Art focusing on Sculpture and Metalsmithing, Computer Science, and Mathematics.

Aliess worked in software development as she enjoyed seeing how things worked. She then embarked on a career in the coffee industry repairing and restoring espresso machine equipment.

Aliess passed away on March 18, 2014.

Contributions may be made in her name to Tree House or Southern Equality.

Mikael Edmonds

Mikael Ken Edmonds of Alexandria, VA, died December 22, 2009 at Georgetown University Hospital following a six-month battle with cancer. He was born March 28, 1974, and grew up in Starkville, MS. Edmonds attended Mississippi State School for Math and Science and graduated in 1992 from Starkville High School. He graduated from the University of Alabama in 1997 with bachelor's degrees in journalism and math. He earned a master's degree in strategic intelligence from American Military University in 2004. From 1998 to 2006, he was employed by the Fayetteville (N.C.) Observer as a Web producer and columnist. Edmonds moved to Army Times Publishing Co., Springfield, Va., in 2006 where he was a Web editor. In 2008, he moved to Northrop Grumman, McLean, Va., where he worked as a social media analyst.

Henry Lee "Trey" Mangum III

Trey earned his Bachelor Degree in 1997 from Millsaps College, a Master of Communication from Mississippi College, a Master of Social Work in 2004 from the University of Texas, and his PhD in Social Work from Jackson State University.

Trey served as the Executive Director of Grace House from 2005-2010 and was the Director of Housing for NO/AIDS Task Force in New Orleans. A communicant of St. James' Episcopal Church, he served as Vice Chairman of the National Episcopal AIDS Coalition.

Jim Owens

Jim Owens, 34, of Kingsport, TN, passed away on Sunday (July 19, 2009).

Born in Pasagoula, MS, he had resided in Kingsport for the past six years. Jim was employed by BAE systems as a research and development chemist. He was a graduate of the University of Southern Mississippi and Loyola University of Chicago. Jim was a member of First Broad Street United Methodist Church. He was a loving father, son and brother. He is survived by his wife, Mandy Owens; son, Graham Owens; and daughter, Anna Owens, all of Kingsport; parents, Clarence and Kathie Owens of Akron, Ohio; and brother, Mark Owens and wife, Deni of Birmingham, Ala.

In lieu of flowers, the family requests that memorial contributions be made to St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, 501 St Jude Pl, Memphis, TN 38105.

Jason Powell
Around 1:30AM, Friday, November 12, 1999, Jason died when a train collided with his car.
Jason Powell ("J.P.") was a great friend. As someone who lived not more than 90 feet from him for two years, I know that he was one of the nicest, friendliest, and most "for-real" people in our class. He always managed to find something good or humorous about whatever life threw at him, and because of that, it seemed that nothing could ever get him down.

He was a leader. If he ever played a sport with you, he made the game fun, because he knew how to handle himself and other people on the field. And, because of that natural charisma, people liked him and wanted him around.

J.P. was a great guy and friend, and I'm glad I had the opportunity to know him while he was with us.

Erik Brown, Class of 1996
I've been putting this off for a while now. I have had a very hard time coping with the loss of such a great friend as Jason. We were roommates for the second half of senior year. But we were much more than roommates, we were very close friends. I have not experienced such a loving group of people as I did with Jason, Heidi, David, Gary, Chris, Rosemary, Amelia, Jeremy, and all the rest of you. Every time I think about Jason, I can't help but smile. He was always strong in his convictions and rarely had a negative word to say about anyone. He was an example to the rest of us. Jason, we miss you dearly and can't wait to see you again.
Shawn Cook, Class of 1996
I have a lot of great memories from MSMS, and among them are the times I would play pool with J.P. in the activity center. J.P. was what you could call a genuinely great person -- smart yet humble, kind, and fun. I will never forget his laugh or his smile. He always made the best of whatever situation came his way.

Everyone who knew J.P. has suffered a great loss with his untimely departure, because everyone who was around him would inevitably have his optimism and joy rub off on them. But nothing makes me sadder than to think that J.P. will not have more time to find joy in life here on earth, something he did so well. I am indebted to him for making such a positive impression on me, and I will always cherish my memories of him.

Mike Joyce, Class of 1996
I really miss Jason. J.P., Chris Fite, and I all went to the same high school. We became instant friends in our Lift Off Program, for gifted students. J.P. was the one responsible for helping me get saved. He invited me out to this big event at Columbia High School and that night I gave my life to God. I will be forever thankful to JP for the great gift. We made a whole lot of trips together from Columbia to Columbus on extended weekends. He was one of the most kindhearted persons that you would ever encounter. He was truly an angel. I MISS YOU J.P. I know that you are smiling down on us.
Reco Williams, Class of 1996
